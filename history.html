Droning About

History

Drones are unmanned aerial vehicles which are controlled by their pilots on the ground through many forms of instruments but mainly a remote control. FPV or first person view flying is the ability of the pilot to control the aircraft from the aircraft’s perspective. The drone has a camera on board along with many receivers and transmitters which transmit live image to the pilot. With basic parelence out of the way, the history of multi-rotor drones begins with the success and popularization of mobile smartphones. Accelerometers and gyroscopes, which are responsible for keeping images on a smartphone upright, became mass produced and affordable for enthusiasts to purchase. In the context of drones they are an instrumental component in maintaining a smooth flight. These advancements coupled with more accessible 3D printing technology has made this a more consumer facing interest to have. While the hobby has advanced in the past 5 years at a breakneck pace, it's fair to say that it's still very young.

There are a variety of form factors that drones can take. From airplane-like fixed-wing crafts to multi-rotor 5-inch freestyle drones like the one on the left of the screen. Drones can be designed and optimized for a variety of tasks but in the FPV context, it’s either racing or freestyle. ‘Racing’ refers to racing drones against one another in a variety of different races.
