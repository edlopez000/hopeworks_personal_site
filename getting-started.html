Getting Started

Practice

Unless the pilot has previous experience, it can be difficult to have a successful first flight that doesn’t begin and end with a wild drone flying around threatening everyone's safety. For this particular reason, it is recommended that you practice in a simulator beforehand. No simulator achieves a perfect simulacrum of real-world flight but they do help in teaching the mechanics of flying with gimbals (also called sticks). The one that is most recommended is Velocidrone. While the simulator can be played using many instruments, it is best to practice with a RC transmitter. But if you want to get experience right now, you can try out Droning About's proprietary simulator software by clicking the link below.

Flying

Flying is all about putting together all the things you learned in your simulator practice hours. You should understand the transmitter well enough to be able to identify how the two sticks control the throttle, yaw, roll, and pitch. In the first couple flying sessions, practicing landing and taking off is key. Losing a drone in your first flight is never fun and neither is having to look for it in a large field. Aside from the mechanics of flying, picking locations that are safe for everyone is also important. Drones can go pretty fast and their propellers spin even faster. It’s important to select locations that are relatively isolated.
